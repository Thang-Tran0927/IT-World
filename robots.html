Robots

When it comes to the state of the art of robotics, human-like robots are undeniably one of the most popular in this sector of technology. Human-like robots, like humans, can accomplish practically everything an ordinary person can do, and the results suggest that the robots even outperform them. One of the most prominent examples would be the use of bartender robots, which could serve up to 300 drinks per hour while an average bartender could only produce about 140 glasses. The former doubled the latter, and as a consequence, we could see the benefits of utilizing robots over people. In contrast, even when synchronized with the most technological facilities, there is still something that robots lack, and that is the understanding of feelings, which can manifest itself in a variety of ways, such as showing empathy, emotions, even humour, and also the recognition that they are only robots. There was an example of a robot with great self-awareness drowning in water after understanding it was simply a tool for humans. So, what will happen in the future if robots can now comprehend emotions and have a high level of self-awareness? Should society see them as human since they possess all of the characteristics that distinguish mankind from others, or will they continue to be regarded as "normal robots"? This is still a controversial subject among scientists since theoretically, robots made of metal do not have a soul like humans, thus treating them as regular beings would be unthinkable. For illustration, if the lives of a robot with a good knowledge of feelings and self-awareness and a typical human being are on the line, who would you end up saving?(1) On the other hand, robots might help us explore locations where humans can not go, such as space exploration. I think that in the next few years, NASA will send more and more robots to investigate the cosmos. For instance, without the aid of the robot rover, we would never know what "life" is like, and the future seems promising in this area. I feel that robots have changed our lives forever, even if just in the most insignificant aspects. In my experience, robots have made my life considerably simpler. For example, because I am usually busy with university work, I hardly have any time to polish my room, but the robot cleaner is the major component in maintaining a clean workplace environment, preserving my health. A small machine like that has already had a substantial influence on my life, so I am convinced that in the future, owing to rapid technological improvements in robotics, humanity's life in general, and mine in particular, will be much simpler. And because robots are designed to make our lives easier, we will start to rely on these types of technology more and more. One of the clearest examples for me would be the robot vacuum, which is obviously beneficial, but at the same time, the user is losing basic essential survival abilities, which contributes to poor physical and mental health and , as well as the development of a poor characteristic. For the time being, robots do not have a significant impact on my life. Of course, I still rely on things such as vending machines or ATMs, but not in the way that my life is entirely dependent on them. And I honestly can't imagine what my life would be like in the next several years when technology takes over the world. To be more precise, America, the technological paradise, has already used an electric car (Tesla) with a self-driving capability, and it is unimaginable how robots will enhance and modify our lives in the future, but they will undoubtedly have a major influence. As I previously indicated, whether it is myself or my family and friends, the influence of this technological area is evident, and everyone should be prepared for the worst/best possibilities, in which they will either lose their work due to robots or their life will be elevated to a whole different level.
